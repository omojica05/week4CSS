Olivia Mojica


About Me

My name is Olivia Mojica. I am a 20-year-old college student in my third year. I was raised in Manhattan my whole life, where I still reside in Washington Heights. Growing up, I always had an affinity for the arts and wanted to pursue them as a career someday. I attended the High School of Fashion Industries in Manhattan, where I majored in Art and Design. In this, I specialized in graphic design, with a minor focus on illustration and photography.

Education

Currently, I attend New York City College of Technology, where I study Communication Design. With this education, I plan to pursue a career in graphic design, specifically doing album cover or movie poster design. I want to make a difference as an artist in this current field of design. The field has been so flooded with corporate minimalism and capitalist work used only to sell something. I want my work to reflect a time when design actually had meaning. I want it to breathe a new life into the design world, reflect the human experience, and be more intentional with the things I create, not just designing for money but for purpose.

Skills

In my educational experience so far, I have learned many basic design techniques that have not only strengthened my designs visually but also my work ethic. I have established principles of typography, alignment, balance, unity, and hierarchy. I have strengthened my illustration abilities through introductory and figure drawing courses. I have learned to collaborate and communicate effectively in design teams and with clients. With this portfolio, I hope to showcase not only my skills but also my strong messaging and communication in my work.

Content

Much of the rest of my portfolio pieces tell a different story. They reflect the more playful side of my art. My medium of choice outside of graphic design is collage. I love how versatile this medium is, from putting pieces and pictures together like a puzzle to carefully cutting small pieces of paper to make a larger image. Much of this work reflects surrealist imagery, dreamlike and illogical, but still carries strong messages of the political goings on around the world. This is something I pride myself on with my work. It is a combination of spontaneity in my imagery and precision in my clear message of prioritizing political struggle.
